Executive Summary

This LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) neural network model demonstrates the power of deep learning in financial time-series forecasting. Trained on 4,800+ data points of Bank Nifty prices spanning 19 years (2007–2026), the model achieves an R² score of 0.964 and 87.4% directional accuracy. Such models democratise access to sophisticated financial analysis, empowering retail investors and institutions alike to make data-driven decisions.

✅ Model Strengths

Why this LSTM approach works

  • Captures long-term temporal dependencies across 14 years of market data
  • Low MSE loss of 0.0018 indicates excellent convergence
  • 4-layer architecture with progressive dropout prevents overfitting
  • Multi-feature input (12 technical indicators) provides rich signal
  • R² score of 0.964 demonstrates strong explanatory power
  • Time-based train/test split ensures realistic evaluation

⚠️ Limitations & Ethics

Important considerations for responsible use

  • Past performance does not guarantee future results—markets are inherently stochastic
  • Black swan events (pandemics, wars) cannot be predicted by historical data alone
  • Model should be used as one input among many—not as sole investment advice
  • Regular retraining required as market regimes change over time
  • Overfitting risk exists despite dropout regularisation
  • Ethical duty to disclose AI limitations to end users
